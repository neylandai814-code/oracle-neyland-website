Methodology & Caveats

This is a first-pass triage list, not a final entitlement memo. Per-parcel listing data (acreage, listed zoning class, listing URL) was cross-verified across two or more aggregators where possible. Owner names, parcel IDs (PINs), exact slope values, FEMA flood zone confirmations, VPD counts, and utility will-serve confirmations are flagged "TBV" — these require county GIS, assessor, DOT, and utility provider lookups during pre-feasibility. Owner phone numbers and PINs were not auto-fabricated.

Inventory caveat: only 12 parcels met the base criteria for the daily list today. SE Sun Belt + N/Central FL inventory is currently tight for ≥200-unit-capable parcels at attractive basis — many strong sites are off-market or under LOI to local merchant builders. Recommend layering broker outreach on top of public listing pulls.

Dear [OWNER]:

This Letter of Intent ("LOI") sets forth the principal terms upon which [BUYER ENTITY], a [STATE] limited liability company ("Purchaser"), proposes to purchase the real property described below from [SELLER ENTITY] ("Seller"). Except for the binding provisions in Section 11 (Confidentiality), Section 12 (Exclusivity), and Section 13 (Brokerage), this LOI is non-binding and creates no obligation on either party until a definitive Purchase and Sale Agreement ("PSA") is fully executed.

1.  PROPERTY
    Address / Parcel(s):  [ADDRESS] · Parcel ID(s) [PIN]
    Acreage:              Approx. [ACRES] acres, more or less, per [SURVEY/TAX RECORD]
    Zoning:               [CURRENT ZONING] — Purchaser intends to develop multifamily under [BY-RIGHT / WITH REZONING TO __]
    Use Restrictions:     Confirm none beyond those of public record

2.  PURCHASE PRICE
    Purchaser shall pay Seller a total purchase price of $[PURCHASE PRICE] ("Purchase Price"), payable in cash at Closing, subject to customary prorations and adjustments. The Purchase Price is not contingent on financing; however, this LOI is contingent on Purchaser's satisfactory completion of due diligence as described below.

3.  EARNEST MONEY DEPOSIT
    Within five (5) business days of the full execution of the PSA, Purchaser shall deposit $[INITIAL DEPOSIT] (the "Initial Deposit") into escrow with [TITLE COMPANY]. The Initial Deposit shall be refundable until expiration of the Inspection Period (defined below). Upon expiration of the Inspection Period (if Purchaser elects to proceed), Purchaser shall deposit an additional $[ADDITIONAL DEPOSIT] ("Additional Deposit"). The combined deposit shall become non-refundable but applicable to the Purchase Price at Closing.

4.  INSPECTION / DUE DILIGENCE PERIOD
    Purchaser shall have ninety (90) days from the Effective Date of the PSA (the "Inspection Period") to conduct, at Purchaser's expense, all investigations Purchaser deems necessary, including but not limited to: title and survey review, Phase I environmental site assessment (and Phase II if recommended), wetlands delineation, geotechnical investigation, zoning verification, utility availability and capacity, traffic studies, market and rent comparables, and architectural / engineering schematic design.

    During the Inspection Period, Purchaser may terminate the PSA for any reason upon written notice to Seller and receive a full refund of the Initial Deposit (less title and escrow costs).

5.  ENTITLEMENT CONTINGENCY PERIOD
    Following the Inspection Period, Purchaser shall have an additional one hundred eighty (180) days (the "Entitlement Period") to obtain all governmental approvals required for Purchaser's intended use, including but not limited to: site plan approval, rezoning if required, conditional use permits, traffic concurrency, and utility will-serve letters. If Purchaser is unable to obtain such approvals despite good-faith efforts, Purchaser may terminate and receive a refund of the Additional Deposit. The Entitlement Period may be extended by [60 / 90] days upon Purchaser's deposit of an additional $[EXTENSION DEPOSIT].

6.  CLOSING
    Closing shall occur within thirty (30) days following the later of: (a) expiration of the Entitlement Period, or (b) Purchaser's written notice that all entitlement contingencies have been satisfied. Time is of the essence.

7.  TITLE AND SURVEY
    Within fifteen (15) days of the Effective Date of the PSA, Purchaser shall obtain a title commitment and ALTA/NSPS Land Title Survey at Purchaser's expense. Seller shall convey by general warranty deed, free and clear of all liens and encumbrances except those expressly approved by Purchaser in writing.

8.  CLOSING COSTS AND PRORATIONS
    Seller shall pay: state deed transfer taxes, owner's title insurance premium, real estate broker commissions, and Seller's attorney fees. Purchaser shall pay: lender's title insurance premium, ALTA survey, recording fees, environmental and engineering reports, and Purchaser's attorney fees. Real estate taxes shall be prorated to the Closing Date.

9.  REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES
    Seller's customary representations and warranties shall include, without limitation: (a) good and marketable title; (b) no pending or threatened litigation, condemnation, or governmental proceedings; (c) no environmental contamination or hazardous substances released to Seller's knowledge; (d) no leases, options, or other agreements encumber the Property; (e) no notice of zoning, building code, or other governmental violation. These representations shall survive Closing for one (1) year.

10. ACCESS
    Purchaser and its representatives shall have the right of reasonable access to the Property for inspection, testing, surveying, and other due diligence activities. Purchaser shall maintain liability insurance and indemnify Seller for any damage caused by Purchaser's investigations.

11. CONFIDENTIALITY (BINDING)
    Seller and Purchaser shall keep the terms of this LOI and any information shared during due diligence confidential, except as required by law or for disclosure to attorneys, accountants, lenders, equity partners, and consultants on a need-to-know basis.

12. EXCLUSIVITY (BINDING)
    From the Effective Date through the earlier of (a) execution of the PSA, or (b) thirty (30) days from the date hereof if no PSA is executed by then, Seller shall not solicit, negotiate, or enter into any agreement with any third party regarding the sale of the Property.

13. BROKERAGE (BINDING)
    [Identify any broker(s) and which party pays commission. Seller shall pay [BROKER FIRM] a commission equal to [X%] of the Purchase Price at Closing, in accordance with the separate listing agreement between Seller and Broker.]

14. DEFINITIVE AGREEMENT
    Within fifteen (15) business days of the full execution of this LOI, the parties shall negotiate in good faith and execute a Purchase and Sale Agreement consistent with the terms hereof. Counsel for Purchaser shall prepare the initial draft.

Oracle Brain — System & Methodology

Persistent state directory at ~/Documents/Oracle Reports/_oracle_brain/. Future Oracle runs read this state at session start and update at session end.

Boot Protocol (start of every run)

  1. Read README.md — protocol explainer
  2. Read config.yaml (v2.0) — scoring weights, focus markets, exclusions, behavior toggles
  3. Read feedback_log.md newest-first — Atlas's outcomes & corrections (authoritative)
  4. Read parcel_ledger.csv — dedup against last 60 days
  5. Read market_intel.md — per-market observations applied as scoring modifiers
  6. Read scoring_calibration.md — weight adjustments from prior outcomes
  7. Read playbook.md — best practices and pitfalls
  8. Create today's dated subfolder YYYY-MM-DD/
  9. Begin daily work using updated rubric and dedup set

Closeout Protocol (end of every run)

  1. Append new parcels to parcel_ledger.csv (status NEW)
  2. Update statuses of any previously-tracked parcels mentioned today
  3. Append new market observations to market_intel.md
  4. If user feedback contradicts prior scoring, append to scoring_calibration.md + update config.yaml
  5. Append methodology learnings to playbook.md
  6. Save run metadata to run_history/YYYY-MM-DD_run_meta.md

Auto-Triggers (built into config v2.0)

  • Comp tracker basis check: for every new parcel, look up market in Comp Tracker. If parcel $/acre > 25% above local recent comps, FLAG.
  • Pipeline oversupply flag: for any submarket with >2,000 units U/C in <250k MSA, discount pro forma rent assumption by 5%.
  • Climate insurance adjustment: for FL coastal / GA coastal / NC coastal / HIGH or EXTREME flood-risk parcels, default insurance to $2,000/unit/yr in pro forma; auto-run INSURANCE 2x scenario.
